Summary & Background

Colette is an MBA Candidate (Class of 2016) at Kellogg School of Management's accelerated 1-year program. She also holds a Master in Management and Hospitality from Cornell University’s renown School of Hotel Administration, serves as the Director of Alumni Relations for Kellogg Hospitality Club, and has a unique international background in hospitality, food & beverage, hotel development, and environmental sustainability.

Most recently, she worked as Manager – Hotel Development & Special Projects for the Sino Group of Hotels, where she conducted feasibility studies and reported directly to the Executive Director & Group General Manager.

Due to her interdisciplinary experience, Colette is adept in project management and can handle multiple project assignments in ad-hoc environments. While some of her most compelling skills are her dynamic personality, creativity, determination, and high attention to detail, she understands the importance of teamwork and customer-centrism, and works well with individuals from varying cultures and professional fields.

Experience

Kellogg School of Management, MBA Candidate 2016
(June 2015 - present)

Kellogg School of Management's One-Year MBA is the most accelerated MBA program of the M7 schools. Candidates with proven experience and strong understanding of business fundamentals commence in June, bypass core classes, and enter directly into the class of second year MBA students. Unlike other One-Year MBA Programs, this path allows students to build on experience and focus on electives pertinent to their professional goals.

Kellogg School of Management has been historically ranked one of the top business schools in the world by Forbes Magazine, BusinessWeek, U.S. News & World Report, and The Economist Intelligence Unit, and is part of the Super Elite M7 business schools.

Sino Group of Hotels, Manager - Hotel Development & Special Projects
(September 2012 - March 2015)

Managed special projects and conducted feasibility studies for hotel and restaurant development in the Asia Pacific, with primary focus on Hong Kong. Project duties included pre-opening and planning for new hotel developments and conversion projects of commercial properties. Reported directly to the Executive Director & Group General Manager.

Prior to this position, Colette also worked as a Project Manager overseeing business development for hotel properties in the Asia Pacific.
